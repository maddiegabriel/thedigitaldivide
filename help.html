How You Can Help!

Help bridge the digital divide in education in Canada.



We have compiled a short list of Canadian non-profit organizations whose missions align with the purpose of this project: to find ways to support Canadian children from lower-income families and make sure they have access to proper technology and quality education.
Pathways to Education
Pathways to education is a Canadian organization that provides youth from low-income communities with the resources they need to graduate from high school and break the cycle of poverty. The Pathways to Education Program is delivered to high school students living in low-income communities across Canada. Students in the program receive a comprehensive set of supports needed to overcome the barriers they face and succeed in school, including food vouchers, after-school tutoring/mentoring, financial support, and career planning.

Learn more about Pathways to Education
reBOOT Canada
reBOOT Canada is a registered charity that has been refurbishing donated computer equipment to provide individuals and non-profits with access to subsidized technology since 1995. reBOOT is committed to making access the internet available to everyone; they have been providing free 24/7 Wi-Fi access in various locations throughout Toronto. In partnership with the First Nations and Inuit Youth Employment Strategy (FNIYES), reBOOT Canada also provides youth tech tool kits and trains young people in computer set up, servicing, and sales.

Learn more about reBOOT Canada
Frontier College
Frontier College is a national charitable literacy organization that works with children and youth to build their literacy and numeracy skills, diminish summer learning loss (which is a drop in student performance between school years), and foster a love of reading that will last a lifetime. Canada Learning Code
Canada Learning Code brings accessible computer science to communities across Canada so everyone can create with technology. They partner with educators, design resources, and deliver learning experiences to all Canadians to ensure digital literacy is for everyone. Their programs include Ladies Learning Code, Girls Learning Code and Kids Learning Code. CLC removes financial barriers to education by allowing participants to pay what they can and offering loaner laptops to communities to be borrowed those in financial need.

Learn more about Canada Learning Code
Computers for Schools Canada
For 25 years, the Computers for Schools Canada program provides computers and other digital devices to assist schools, libraries, eligible low-income Canadians, Indigenous communities and not-for-profit organizations. The CFS program has made some significant changes to make it even easier for all Canadians, including youth and under-represented groups, to access the internet and acquire the skills and training needed to work in todayâs digital economy. In 2015, the program successfully delivered 7,500 computers to Syrian Refugees and recently committed to deliver 50,000 computers to low-income families via the new Connecting Families Initiative (CFI). CFS also helps extend the useful life of electronic equipment which reduces the environmental impact of electronic waste and keeps e-waste out of landfills.
