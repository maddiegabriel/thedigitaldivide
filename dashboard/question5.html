Question 5

Is there a statistically significant difference between the academic performance of lower income households VS non-lower income households due to COVID-19?

Time Spent Leaning Overview
Welch Two Sample T-Test
  • For this question we will use a simple Welch Two Sample T-Test using the t.test() function in R.
  • Our hypothesis will be that the academic performance for students of lower and non-lower income households is the same.
  • We will conduct a two-sided test to cover all possibilities.
  • We will assume variances to be unequal because we are using data from two different groups. Also we do not know if our variances are the same or not.
  • We will set our null hypothesis as 0. Which really means that our mean difference is 0.
  • For this t-test we will use 95% as our confidence interval.
  • For the results of this test, if our p-value is less than 0.05 we will assume that the mean for two groups is significantly different. As such we will conclude that there is a statistically significant difference between the academic performance of students from lower income households versus the non-lower income households.
  • On the other hand if our p-value is equal to or greater than 0.05 we will conclude that there is no significant statistical difference between academic performance of students from lower income households versus non-lower income households.

Results & Analysis

From question 4 we already know that there is no significant staistical change in the study time for students with non low income families. As such even though we consuducted t-test #1 to find out whether there was a stastical difference between the study times for student from low income and non low income families. Which turned out to be significant. Next we conducted t-test #2 to find out if there was a significant statistical difference between the study times for low and non low income families and it turned out to be significant. Although, we already know that there was no significant change in the study time for non low income families during COVID-19 and prior to it. As such, we cannot conclude that low income families are more impacted by COVID-19 in terms of education. The purpose of our conducting the 2 t-tests was to be able to compare the two test to see the cahnges in the values of the t-tests. Just to keep in mind that the greater the t-value the greater the stastical significance and the smaller the p-value the greater the statistical significance. The t-value for t-test #2 is smaller than for t-test #1 and the p-value for t-test #2 is greater than the p-value for t-test #1. As such, we can conclude the stastical significance has decreassed from t-test #1 to t-test #2 which should have increased atleast according to our hypothesis. Due to this we have to further reject our hypothesis that COVID-19 had a more significant impact on the education of students from lower income families than compared to non lower income families.

Data Limitations

In terms of data our limitations are the same as the ones mentioned in questions 3 and 4. As mentioned before our datasets are very limited due to the fact that usually data is only made available for any given year after the year has ended or at the very end of the year as a sort of reflection. Also, COVID-19 has given rise to new issues and challenges as such data regarding those issues and challenges isn’t readily available. The datasets that we have used for questions 3 and 4 are taken from the UK and while there are a lot of similarities between Canada and the UK there are also many differences. As such, we fear that our data may not fully reflect the situation in Canada.

Data Assumptions

We have 2 data sets and we want to find out if the two are statistically different or not. As such using a t-test is our best option to try and understand how similar or different the two datasets are and what kind of conclusion we could draw from them. Furthermore, we are using the p-value of 0.05 to indicate whether the 2 groups of data are statistically different or not. Although 0.05 is a standard value used by a lot of researchers, it doesn’t give us a complete hundred percent guarantee that our conclusion is correct. Rather we have to assume that our conclusion is correct.
