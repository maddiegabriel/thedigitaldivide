Methodology

For Question 4, we used similar methodology to Question 3. We performed Welch's two sample T-Test to compare the average academic performance of non-low SES from before the pandemic to during. Our null hypothesis is that the average time spent studying between the non-low SES pandemic vs pre-pandemic group is equal. Our alternate hypothesis is that the average time spent studying between the non-low SES pandemic vs pre-pandemic group is statistically significantly different.

Results

Our results indicate there is not enough evidence to reject the null hypothesis. The t-test provides a very large p-value, as a result, we cannot conclude if the change in study time before vs during COVID-19 is statistically significant. The results of this question will be analyzed alongside the results of Question 3 and 5 to determine if the study time of non-lower income households is statistically different than lower income households and if low-income populations are more vulnerable to changes than their higher income counterparts.

Limitations

This question possesses similar limitations as Question 3, which include:

The source had unequal sample sizes for the pre COVID and during COVID populations. Although we had substantial data points for pre COVID, the during COVID group only had 4 observations. We could have greater confidence in our study with a larger sample size.

A second limitation is that we used two different sources to gather data regarding study time before vs during COVID. These two sources had differences in data collection methodology, and had slight differences in what qualifies as study time.

These sources were also international as opposed to Canada specific. The IFS data was primarily from the UK, and the OECD drew from a variety of international countries including the UK and Canada. This makes it more challenging to apply the findings to a Canadian context.

Finally, it is important to note that non low-income students include medium and high income students. Our results will be different than if we observed each socioeconomic class independently.
