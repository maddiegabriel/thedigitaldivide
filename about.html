About the Project

Welcome to our term project for CIS*4020 (Data Science)! This dashboard was created through a semester-long project for a course in the School of Computer Science at the University of Guelph. The project is based around the UN Sustainable Development Goals, specifically Goal #4: Quality Education.

The goal of this project is to analyze and display data pertaining to the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on lower-income Canadian families. We specifically look childrens' access to quality education as defined by their digital access, digital literacy, and time spent learning at home.

Through this project, we investigate to what extent lower-income Canadian families may be disproportionately impacted by COVID-19 compared to their middle and high income counterparts. Our goal is to help people gain a better understanding of the equitablability of educational services across socioeconomic statuses in Canada; in particular, we hope to show how the delivery of this essential services for lower-income families has been altered or disrupted by the COVID-19 pandemic. As a result of our research and amalgamation of relevant data, we hope to have found evidence to encourage the development of increased support and services for children from lower-income households in Canada, who are facing enormous challenges due to the fallout from the COVID-19 pandemic.

Please start by navigating to The Issue page to get an introduction to the project topic. After that, you can navigate through our dashboards to view the data used to answer each question, learn about the methods we employed to analyze that data, and view the results and findings from these analyses.
